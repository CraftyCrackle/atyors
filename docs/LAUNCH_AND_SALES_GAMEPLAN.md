# atyors.com — Launch & Sales Gameplan

> **Product:** Curbside services (Phase 1: trash barrel put-out/bring-in)  
> **Audience:** Homeowners & property managers (Boston area focus)  
> **Status:** Live at atyors.com; iOS app in UAT.

---

## 1. Positioning & Message

**One-liner:**  
*“Never forget trash day again — we put your barrels out and bring them in, on your schedule.”*

**Who it’s for:**  
- Busy homeowners who travel or work long hours  
- Seniors or anyone who finds barrels heavy or inconvenient  
- Property managers with multiple units  
- People who’ve had barrels stolen or blown over and want a reliable routine  

**Why now:**  
- Recurring, predictable need (weekly pickup)  
- Clear outcome: no missed pickups, no dragging barrels  
- Simple to explain and buy (one-time or subscription)

**Key differentiators to stress:**  
- You choose the schedule (recurring or one-off)  
- Live tracking so you know when it’s done  
- Transparent pricing, pay per service or subscribe

## 2. Launch Phases

### Phase A — Soft launch (first 4–6 weeks)

**Goal:** Prove demand and operations in 1–2 towns; fix what breaks.

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Pick 1–2 pilot towns (e.g. Everett + one other) where you can reliably serve | PM |
| 2 | Turn on service zones in the app for those towns only | Dev |
| 3 | Recruit 1–3 servicers (friends, gig workers, local handymen) for the pilot | PM |
| 4 | Set simple pricing (e.g. $X per put-out, $Y per bring-in, $Z both) and add to landing page / book flow | PM + Dev |
| 5 | Create a short “pilot” page: “Now available in [Town]. Sign up and get your first week at [discount].” | PM + Dev |
| 6 | Drive 20–50 signups via personal network, local Facebook groups, Nextdoor, flyers | PM |
| 7 | Run 2–4 weeks of real jobs; track no-shows, complaints, servicer feedback | PM |
| 8 | Iterate: pricing, messaging, onboarding, app flow | All |

**Success:**  
- 10+ paying customers completing at least 2 service dates  
- No critical operational or app failures  
- Clear list of “must fix” and “nice to have” before wider launch  

---

### Phase B — Local launch (weeks 6–12)

**Goal:** Grow in the pilot area and add 1–2 adjacent towns.

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Add 1–2 more towns to service zones based on servicer capacity | PM |
| 2 | Publish a simple “Where we serve” page (list of towns + “Request my town”) | Dev |
| 3 | Double down on channels that worked in Phase A (e.g. Nextdoor, local FB, flyers) | PM |
| 4 | Offer a referral incentive (e.g. “Give $10, get $10” or one free week) | PM + Dev |
| 5 | Collect testimonials and short quotes; add to site and social | PM |
| 6 | Optional: partner with 1–2 HOAs or property managers for multi-address pilots | PM |
| 7 | Monitor unit economics: revenue per job, servicer pay, time per stop | PM |

**Success:**  
- 50+ active customers  
- Repeat rate and retention improving  
- At least one testimonial or case study you can reuse  

---

### Phase C — Scale & repeat (month 3+)

**Goal:** Expand geography and channels; systematize sales and ops.

| Step | Action | Owner |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Add towns in a repeatable way (map + “Request my area” → prioritize by demand) | PM + Dev |
| 2 | Hire or contract more servicers per zone; define simple training/onboarding | PM |

| 5 | Revisit pricing (seasonality, multi-barrel, discounts for annual) | PM |

## 3. How to Sell It (Channels & Tactics)

### Direct (you or a part-time sales person)

- **In person:** Farmers’ markets, town events, HOA meetings. Demo the app (schedule, tracking), sign people up on the spot or capture email/phone for follow-up.
- **Door-to-door (pilot towns):** Flyer + “We’re new in [Town] — first week half off.” Leave a clear CTA (QR or atyors.com/book).
- **Phone/email:** Follow up every “Request my town” and every abandoned signup with a short, helpful message and a simple offer (e.g. first week free).

### Organic (low cost)

- **Nextdoor:** Post in pilot towns: “New service in [Town]: we put your barrels out and bring them in. Here’s how it works.” Link to atyors.com. Reply to every comment.
- **Facebook:** Local town/community groups. Same message; avoid sounding like an ad — lead with the problem (forgot trash day, back pain, travel).
- **Google Business Profile:** Create/claim atyors.com listing; add service area, hours, photos, and link to book.
- **SEO:** One page per town when you have capacity (e.g. “Trash barrel service in Everett”). Title: “Trash Barrel Put-Out & Bring-In | [Town] | atyors”.

### Paid (when you have proof it works)

- **Google Ads:** Keywords like “trash barrel service [town]”, “someone to put trash cans out”, “curbside trash service”. Start with $10–20/day, track cost per signup.
- **Facebook/Instagram:** Target by zip in pilot areas; creative = “Never miss trash day” + CTA to atyors.com.
- **Referral:** “Give $10, get $10” (or one free week). Implement in app and mention in every email and on the dashboard.

### Partnerships (medium effort, high trust)

- **Property managers:** “We handle barrel put-out/bring-in for your tenants — one less complaint and one less task for you.”
- **Real estate agents:** “Offer new buyers a month of barrel service as a move-in perk.”
- **Senior / aging-in-place programs:** “We help seniors stay independent; we handle the barrels.”

## 4. Pricing & Packaging (Starter Ideas)

Keep it simple at launch:

| Offer | Example | Use case |
|-------|--------|----------|
| One-time put-out | $15 | “I’m away this week.” |
| One-time bring-in | $15 | Same |
| Both (one-time) | $25 | One-off |
| Weekly recurring (put-out + bring-in) | $20–30/week or $80–120/month | Core subscription |

- Show price clearly in the book flow and on the pilot page.  
- Consider: first week 50% off or free for pilot signups.  
- Later: multi-barrel pricing, annual discount.

## 5. Metrics to Watch

| Metric | Target (Phase A) | Why |
|--------|-------------------|-----|
| Signups (pilot) | 20–50 | Enough to stress-test ops |
| Conversion (visit → booked) | Track baseline; aim to improve | Messaging and UX |
| Completed jobs per customer | ≥ 2 | Validates retention |
| Servicer no-show / cancel rate | &lt; 5% | Operational health |
| Cost per acquisition (CPA) | Track by channel | So you know what to scale |
| Revenue per servicer hour | Track | Unit economics |

## 7. Summary

1. **Position** atyors as the easy, reliable way to never worry about trash day.  
2. **Launch small:** 1–2 towns, 1–3 servicers, 20–50 customers; prove operations and messaging.  
3. **Sell** via Nextdoor, local Facebook, flyers, and personal network first; add paid and partnerships once you have proof.  
4. **Measure** signups, completed jobs, retention, and cost per acquisition.  
5. **Expand** town by town and add referrals and simple paid ads as you scale.
